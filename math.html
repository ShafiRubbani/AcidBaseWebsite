pH and pOH Calculations

There are five equations that govern the calculation of pH and pOH. The first, as we already discussed, is that given the hydrogen ion concentration (in molars) [H+] of a solution, pH = -1*log10[H+]. Because exponentiation is the inverse of the logarithm we can also say that given the pH, [H+] (in molars) = 10-pH. This is the second equation. The third and the fourth follow from the fact that the pOH of a solution is (put simply) the pH with hydroxide ions. Given a hydroxide ion concentration (in molars) [OH-] of a solution, pOH = 1*log10[OH-]. Given the pOH, [OH-] (in molars) = 10-pOH. Lastly, the fifth equation is that pH + pOH = 14. Using these five equations, it is possible to ascertain all of the above values given only one.
